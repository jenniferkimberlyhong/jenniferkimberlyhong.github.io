The Challenge

Understanding the Problem

Students and Professionals are utilizing various cloud storage products on a daily basis to create documents, share files, or collaborate on projects and manage on their phones or computer. There are many competitors in the market but users are finding it a difficult, confusing user experience to organize, bookmark, and share with their team.

The Solution

Simple and Collaborative Experience

everyday strives to provide users one place to easily store, organize, manage, and share files on the cloud. Users are able to find inspriation and bring their creativity into life to collaborate with their team.

SECTION I | DISCOVERY & RESEARCH

Competitive Analysis

Turning weaknesses into strengths

The cloud storage market is saturated with big name competitors. To understand this market and its competitors, I researched 3 of the top cloud storage products that are vastly different but have unique characteristics that make it successful: Evernote, Google Drive, and Pinterest. To compete in this market, the product will need to provide functions that are important to the targeted users. A successful product will be a combination of the three with the ability to collaborate seamlessly in real time and create actionable items from bookmarked items.

SECTION III | WIREFRAMES & TESTING

Low Fidelity Wireframes

The Foundation

After creating the information architecture, I began creating low fidelity wireframes and transferring it onto Figma. I started wireframing the landing page, it is where users will create an account and learn about the product and application of everyday and the most important as it is the first impression to new users.

After the usability testing, I was able to see that my 2 participants were able to get through the 3 processes somewhat easily.

  • Some buttons are not going to the associated page when trying to add a new piece of content
  • I see the buttons there but cannot move onto next page
  • Easy to find the navigation to create new content since its directly on the person's home page - quick / easy
  • Confused where to click for organizing content - make it more intuitive
  • Click on move button for organizing content but did not know what to do after
  • Maybe add another window when clicking on Move instead of just the folders showing available

SECTION V | VISUAL DESIGN

High Fidelity Mockup

Preference Testing

Usability Tests are a great way to see participants utilize the website and see if it is intuitive or if it’s challenging for them to navigate throughout the site. Based on the 3 tests performed, I will be updating the design based on feedback. I received questions on page navigation, missing steps, and design decisions. These feedback are great for me to go back and understand where the struggles are for users. When I’m creating these sometimes, I do tend to forget a few steps since I understand what I’m doing but it’s good to see from another perspective. I also do agree that I can use updates to the design especially with the use of colors and images. And the other top item of page navigation, I believe there are few places on the website that need easier/faster navigation than clicking on the logo. Based on this test, I'll be going back to my hi-fi mock-up and updating based on user feedback as well as feedback from the preference tests.
